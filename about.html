The name “Bliss” came naturally from how I show up around people calm communication, positive energy, and how I connect with others in a simple, human way.

How I Started

I started out in graphic design around early 2022.

At the time, I was working in an electrical company and needed a design for a personal brand idea. I reached out to a designer, but the cost didn’t make sense to me at the time. That moment pushed me to try and learn design myself instead of outsourcing it.

“That’s how I got into graphic design not from inspiration, but from necessity.”

As I learned, I began to understand layout, composition, and how visual decisions affect communication. But over time, I realized something important.

Good-looking designs were not enough. Many products looked visually fine but still failed because users couldn’t understand them or didn’t enjoy using them.

That realization shifted my direction. I didn’t just want to design visuals I wanted to design products that actually work. That’s what led me into product design around 2024, and by 2025 I fully committed to it.

What I Do Now

Today, I focus on designing user-centered digital products with clarity and structure.

  • SaaS product interfaces
  • Mobile and web applications
  • Dashboards and internal tools
  • UX research and interface design
  • Design systems and prototyping (Figma)

I approach every project with one goal: Make it easy to understand, easy to use, and hard to forget.
